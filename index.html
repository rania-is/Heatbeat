CLIMATE-WISE EXERCISE PROJECT

Shaping the Future of Exercise in a Warming World

About the Project

The Challenge

Have you noticed how your regular running route feels harder on hot days? Or how your usual workout leaves you more exhausted during summer? You're not alone. As our climate changes, we need to adapt how we stay active - but current fitness apps and training programs haven't caught up with this new reality.

The Reality

Our warming world isn't just changing the environment - it's transforming how our bodies respond to exercise. When you exercise in heat, your body faces a double challenge: managing the stress from the workout itself while also trying to keep cool. Yet most fitness technology treats every day the same, regardless of the temperature outside.

Our Research

The HeatBeat project studies how environmental heat affects exercise performance. We combine multiple aspects of exercise science: physiological monitoring, environmental factors, and contextual elements. Our research focuses on real-world exercise situations, not just laboratory conditions, because we know that's where the real challenge lies.

The Vision

Our research examines the relationship between heat stress and exercise to develop better activity guidelines for warmer conditions. The findings will contribute to more effective exercise recommendations that consider both environmental conditions and personal factors.

Join the Study

By participating in this study, you're contributing to important research that will help shape how we exercise in a changing climate. Together, we can find better ways to keep people moving safely and confidently, regardless of the temperature outside.

To join this study, you need to be:

• At least 18 years old

• Residing in Europe

• Proficient in English

• Have a Garmin watch

• Have a smartphone (iOS or Android)

• Respond to daily surveys (<1 minute each)

• Share data from your smartphone

• Share data from your Garmin smartwatch

The study runs from August 1st to August 31st, 2025.

By participating, you'll be advancing research on exercise in our changing climate. At the end of the study, you'll receive a detailed report showing your personal exercise patterns and how environmental conditions affect your performance.

We take your privacy seriously:

• All data is anonymized and stored securely

• Compliant with GDPR regulations

• Used only for research purposes

• You can drop out of the study and request data deletion at any time

Participation in this research is completely voluntary. You can withdraw from the study at any time, request deletion of your data, and face no negative consequences for doing so.
